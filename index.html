About

Euclid QSO Working Package

The Euclid space telescope, launched by the European Space Agency (ESA) in July 2023, is designed to map the large-scale structure of the Universe across an unprecedented wide-field survey. The QSO Working Package (WP) focuses on detecting, characterising, and scientifically exploiting the population of quasars — in particular at high redshift (z > 2.5).

This public portal provides access to data products, catalogues, and scientific results produced by the Euclid QSO team. Our work spans photometric selection, spectroscopic confirmation, and multi-wavelength analysis of quasar candidates across the Euclid wide-field and deep surveys.

Key survey characteristics

  • VIS optical photometry (550–900 nm)
  • NISP near-infrared photometry (Y, J, H bands)
  • NISP slitless spectroscopy (0.92–1.89 µm)
  • Wide Survey: ≈ 14,000 deg² to mAB ≈ 24.5
  • Deep Survey: ≈ 53 deg² to mAB ≈ 26.5
  • Euclid Auxiliary Fields for calibration

Data releases follow the Euclid Consortium publication policy.

Science Goals

What We Are Investigating

The Euclid QSO team pursues a wide range of science objectives, from cosmological probes to the study of the earliest epochs of black-hole growth.

🕳️

Supermassive Black Hole Growth

Characterising the quasar luminosity function and black-hole mass function across cosmic time, tracing the build-up of the most massive black holes.

🌅

Epoch of Reionisation Probes

Detecting quasars at z > 6 to probe the intergalactic medium during and after the epoch of hydrogen reionisation.

📐

Large-Scale Structure

Using the quasar angular power spectrum and 3D clustering to constrain cosmological parameters and test dark energy models.

🔦

Quasar–Host Co-evolution

Leveraging Euclid's spatial resolution to measure the morphology and stellar masses of quasar host galaxies across cosmic history.

⚗️

Spectral Energy Distributions

Combining Euclid photometry with external surveys (LSST, eROSITA, WISE) to build multi-wavelength SEDs and constrain physical quasar properties.

🧭

Photometric Selection

Developing and validating machine-learning and template-fitting pipelines for robust photometric quasar selection and redshift estimation.
